# Cultural Context: 比爱心 (bi ai xin)

## Meaning

比爱心 (bǐ ài xīn) literally translates to "make a heart" or "compare hearts." It is a hand gesture where both hands are raised — typically above or in front of the head — with the fingertips or wrists touching to form the shape of a heart.

## Cultural Significance

The 比爱心 gesture is widely recognized across Chinese and broader East Asian popular culture. It is used to express:

- **Love and affection** — between friends, family, or romantic partners
- **Gratitude and appreciation** — toward fans, audiences, or supporters
- **Goodwill and positivity** — as a warm, non-verbal greeting

The gesture gained mainstream popularity through Chinese social media platforms (Weibo, Douyin/TikTok), K-pop fan culture, and celebrity interactions with audiences. It is commonly seen at concerts, livestreams, photo sessions, and casual social encounters.

Unlike formal traditional gestures, 比爱心 is informal and contemporary. It carries no hierarchical or ceremonial connotations — it is simply a universal expression of warmth and positive emotion.

## Gesture Description

1. Both arms are raised above or in front of the head
2. The hands come together with fingertips touching, forming the top of a heart shape
3. The arms and elbows form the curved sides of the heart
4. The gesture is held briefly (1-3 seconds) before lowering the arms

Variations exist: some people form smaller hearts using just the fingers (e.g., thumb-and-index finger heart from K-pop), but the full-arm version described above is the most visually expressive and suitable for whole-body motion capture.
